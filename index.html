Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time and see how innovation, energy efficiency, and responsible manufacturing shaped a more sustainable future for technology and the planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel begins with a new name, a new vision, and a strong foundation in semiconductor innovation.

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, starting a company that would help define modern computing.

1971

First Microprocessor

The Intel 4004 launches the microprocessor era and changes computing forever.

Intel debuts the world's first commercial microprocessor, creating a breakthrough that powers the future of personal and business computing.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 helps establish the x86 architecture that still shapes computing today.

This processor becomes a key foundation for countless PCs and servers, creating a lasting architecture for the industry.

1985

386 Processor

The 386 brings 32-bit computing to personal computers and improves performance.

Intel introduces a chip that helps PCs handle more powerful software and smoother multitasking.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions before accelerating long-term improvements.

The company invests in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and better manufacturing efficiency to reverse this trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and sets goals for climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

The strategy focuses on responsible and inclusive progress while helping drive sustainability across the industry.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces a goal to reach net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.

This commitment builds on years of environmental initiatives and pushes Intel toward a cleaner future.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

This milestone lowers carbon emissions and supports Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel brings partners together to share ideas for next-generation sustainable manufacturing.

The summit unites suppliers, officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on the future of semiconductor sustainability.
